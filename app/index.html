I am David Greenwood-Sánchez, and I’m running for City Council in Ward 5.

I am a child of St. Paul – I live in the Como neighborhood, and I’ve spent most of my life here. I attended MMSA, Central High School, and the University of Minnesota Humphrey School. I also hold a PhD in political science from UW-Madison. Currently, I serve our community as a board member of the St. Paul Central Foundation and as co-founder of the Twin Cities Front Yard Organic Gardener’s Club.

I’m running for council because I’m concerned that the current brand of politics is damaging the soul of our city – our neighborhoods. I saw this firsthand in my neighborhood with the destruction of the historic St. Andrews church building, a political decision made from the top down that excluded community members and left our community divided. Unfortunately, we are seeing this model of top-down decision-making across the city, leaving many of us to ask: Where is our voice? Who will stand up in support of our neighborhoods? Who is listening to us?

I’m also running in opposition to the proposed sales tax increase, which disproportionately hurts our low-income families and individuals. The sales tax (approaching 10%) is also harmful for local businesses, because it encourages buyers to go to other cities for shopping. Let’s be clear: Making basic road maintenance contingent upon tax increases is bad governance. It is unfair to taxpayers and damages our city’s long-term health and reputation. I promise to work for a more responsible pathway forward.

St. Paul needs new leadership – We need real leaders who know our communities, who understand our needs, and who can articulate a vision for the future based on values of social inclusion and fiscal responsibility. I promise to be a champion of affordable housing, our public schools, and our environment. I will also offer a new voice to help represent our growing Latino communities, which have historically been left out of our city’s political decision-making.

I ask that you consider voting for me on November 7th. I’ve dedicated my career to public policy and to my community. I helped lead the effort to reverse Falcon Heights’ ban on front yard gardening, I was part of the successful fight to protect Pig’s Eye Lake from harmful contamination, and I worked alongside my neighbors to defend historic buildings across the city. Professionally, I teach political science, specializing in environmental politics and Latin American politics. I have also worked as an economic researcher for Poverty Action Lab in Santiago, Chile, and as a program evaluator for the MN Office of the Legislator Auditor.

I believe you will see that I am a genuine person, motivated by love for my city, my community, and my neighbors. I look forward to meeting you, hearing from you, and getting to know you. In the meantime, please reach out to me at votedavidgs@gmail.com. And most importantly, mark your calendar for Nov. 7th.

Thank you kindly,

David Greenwood-Sánchez
